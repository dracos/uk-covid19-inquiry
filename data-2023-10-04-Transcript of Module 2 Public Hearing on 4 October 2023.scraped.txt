12                     My Lady, I want to start in 1924, when the League of
13              Nations adopted the Declaration of the Rights of the
14              Child in Geneva. That declaration was drafted by
15              Eglantyne Jebb, who had founded Save the Children Fund
16              in London in 1919 along with her sister Dorothy Buxton,
17              to provide relief to children suffering poverty and
18              starvation following the First World War.
19                     That declaration was drafted in recognition that
20              children have rights that are important and distinct
21              from adults. That declaration later inspired the 1989
22              United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child,
23              itself a landmark international agreement that enshrines
24              the civil, political, social, economic and cultural
25              rights of children, and has become the most widely
    1

 1             ratified human rights treaty in history.
